# Working Backwards FAQ Template

For calibration, see `references/example-faq.md`.

## Mode Guidance
- `Lite`: answer 6-8 questions total. Cover customer value, technical feasibility, and risk at minimum.
- `Standard`: answer 8-10 questions total. Cover each category at least once, and keep answers tight.
- `Deep`: answer all 12 core questions, then add 3-5 context-specific questions if they sharpen the decision.

Use the core questions below as a menu in `Lite`/`Standard`, not a mandate to answer every one.

## Customer Value
1. What painful behavior changes if this succeeds?
2. Why will users trust this enough to adopt it?
3. What is the fastest time-to-value for a new user?

## Business Viability
4. What strategic priority does this advance?
5. What metric movement justifies continued investment?
6. What happens if adoption is slower than expected?

## Technical Feasibility
7. What is the smallest viable technical slice?
8. What dependencies could block delivery?
9. What can we de-scope without harming the customer outcome?

## Risk and Governance
10. How could this fail users or erode trust?
11. What guardrails are mandatory before broad launch?
12. What kill criteria will stop this project?

## Add More Hard Questions
- `Lite`/`Standard`: add only the context-specific questions that materially change the decision.
- `Deep`: add 3-5 context-specific questions a skeptical executive or customer would ask.
- Include at least 2 explicit kill/stop questions in `Lite`/`Standard`, or at least 3 in `Deep`.

## Checklist (Pass All)

- `Lite`: 6-8 serious questions are answered.
- `Standard`: 8-10 serious questions are answered.
- `Deep`: all 12 core questions are answered, plus any needed context-specific questions.
- At least 2 kill or stop questions are explicit in `Lite`/`Standard`, or at least 3 in `Deep`.
- At least one likely adoption or trust risk is named.
- At least one real scope, sequencing, or policy decision is recorded.
- At least one kill criterion is explicit.
