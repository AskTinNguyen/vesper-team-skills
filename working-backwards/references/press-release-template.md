# Working Backwards Press Release Template

For calibration, see `references/example-press-release.md`.

## Mode Guidance
- `Lite`: keep this to a short announcement with only the essential sections.
- `Standard`: keep this concise. Prefer one short paragraph plus supporting bullets, and avoid repeating details that belong in the FAQ.
- `Deep`: complete the full template depth.

## Headline
One line with the customer outcome, not the feature mechanics.

## Subheadline
Who this helps and what becomes newly possible.

## Dateline
`[City] - [Month Day, Year]`

## Opening Paragraph
- State the customer problem in plain language.
- State the new outcome now possible.
- State why this matters now.

## Customer Problem (Before)
- What users had to do before.
- Where friction, risk, delay, or confusion lived.

## New Experience (After)
- What changed in user behavior.
- Core flow in 3-5 sentences.
- Why trust and control improved.

## Example Use Case
- One realistic scenario from trigger to outcome.

## Customer Quote
- Role + realistic quote focused on impact.

## Team or Product Quote
- Explain intent and design principle.

## Availability
- Who gets access, in what phase, with what limits.

## Call to Action
- What users should do next.

## Checklist (Pass All)

- Names the target segment explicitly.
- States a clear before/after behavior change.
- Includes at least one measurable improvement or target.
- Includes an explicit trust, control, or safety statement.
- Includes availability or scope limits.
- In `Lite`/`Standard`, the draft stays concise enough to read quickly without feeling like a launch blog post.
