# Sales Quick Reference
## *Print This. Keep It Handy.*

---

## The 5-Second Pitch

> "We turn your team's best practices into shareable AI skills — so every engineer feels confident, aligned, and proud of what they ship."

---

## Before Every Call, Ask Yourself

1. What feeling is this prospect experiencing now? _(pain)_
2. What feeling do they want instead? _(desire)_
3. What story can I tell that bridges them? _(transformation)_

---

## The Opening (First 60 Seconds)

**Don't:** "Let me tell you about our product..."

**Do:** "Before we get into solutions, I want to understand — when your team sits down to tackle a new project, what's the feeling in the room?"

**Then:** Listen. Reflect. Ask "Tell me more about that feeling..."

---

## Discovery Questions (The Feeling Audit)

| Instead of This | Ask This |
|-----------------|----------|
| "What tools do you use?" | "How does your team feel about their current workflow?" |
| "How do you handle reviews?" | "How confident do you feel hitting 'merge' on Friday?" |
| "What's your onboarding process?" | "How do new hires feel at the end of week one?" |
| "Do you use AI assistants?" | "What's the gap between what AI promises and how your team actually feels?" |

---

## Bridge to Demo (The Setup)

> "Imagine it's 3 months from now. [Paint scenario]. What's different is [transformation]. How does that feel?"

**Example:**
> "Imagine it's 3 months from now. A junior engineer who joined yesterday just shipped their first PR using your team's standard workflow. No one told them how — it was just there. What's different is your wisdom is encoded and automatic. How does that feel?"

**Wait for them to describe the feeling.** That's the buying signal.

---

## Demo Script (3 Minutes)

**Minute 1 — The Problem:**
> "Here's a task that usually takes hours and creates that [frustration/anxiety] you mentioned..."

**Minute 2 — The Skill:**
> "Watch this... [execute command]. Notice how it feels, not just what it does."

**Minute 3 — The Feeling:**
> "That took 3 minutes. But what I really want to know — did you feel that sense of [target feeling]?"

---

## The Close (Soft, Feeling-Based)

> "If we could give your team that feeling of [desired state] — [confidence/flow/pride] — would it be worth exploring?"

**If yes:** Schedule next step immediately.
**If maybe:** "What feeling is holding you back?"
**If no:** "Totally understand. What would need to change for this to feel right?"

---

## Objection Responses (30 Seconds Each)

### "Too expensive"
> "I hear that. What's the emotional cost of not solving this? How will your team feel in 6 months if nothing changes?"

### "Need to evaluate options"
> "Absolutely. As you evaluate, pay attention to how each option makes you *feel*. That's what your team will experience daily."

### "Not a priority"
> "I get it. What's the feeling making this wait — are things okay enough, or are other fires burning?"

### "We can build it ourselves"
> "You can. The question is: how will your team feel *building* it vs. *using* it? Building = more decisions. Using = flow."

## Words to Use (Feeling Language)

| ✅ Use These | ❌ Avoid These |
|--------------|----------------|
| Confident, capable, proud | Features, functionality |
| Aligned, in flow, effortless | Integration, API |
| Clear, empowered, natural | Scalable, robust |
| Masterful, certain, light | Enterprise-grade, turnkey |

---

## The 3 Follow-Up Rule

1. **Follow-up 1:** Add value (case study, relevant insight)
2. **Follow-up 2:** Acknowledge silence ("I know you're busy...")
3. **Follow-up 3:** Soft close ("One last try...")

**Never more than 3.** Leave the door open.

---

## Post-Call Checklist

- [ ] Did they use feeling words unprompted?
- [ ] Did I reflect their pain back accurately?
- [ ] Did they describe the future state in their own words?
- [ ] Did I leave them feeling better than I found them?
- [ ] Is the next step scheduled with clear ownership?

---

## Emergency Phrases (When Stuck)

> "Help me understand what you're feeling..."

> "Tell me more about that..."

> "What I'm hearing is... [reflect]. Does that sound right?"

> "If nothing changed, how would your team feel in 6 months?"

> "What would need to be true for this to feel like a no-brainer?"

**My job:** Make them feel understood, hopeful, and capable.
