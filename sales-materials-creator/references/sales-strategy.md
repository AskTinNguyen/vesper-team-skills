## THE SALES CONVERSATION ARCHITECTURE

### Phase 1: Discovery (The Feeling Audit)

**Goal:** Understand their emotional state, not just their technical needs.

#### Questions That Reveal Feelings:

**Instead of:** "What tools are you using?"
**Ask:** "When your team sits down to start a new feature, what's the feeling in the room?"

**Instead of:** "How do you handle code reviews?"
**Ask:** "How confident do you feel when you hit 'merge' on a Friday afternoon?"

**Instead of:** "What's your onboarding process?"
**Ask:** "How does a new engineer feel at the end of their first week?"

**Instead of:** "Do you use AI coding assistants?"
**Ask:** "What's the gap between what your AI tools promise and how your team actually feels using them?"

#### The Magic Follow-Up:

After they answer any question:
> "Tell me more about that feeling..."

**Listen for:**
- Emotional language (frustrated, overwhelmed, proud, confident)
- Stories about specific moments
- What they wish they felt instead

---

### Phase 2: The Mirror (Reflecting Their Pain)

**Goal:** Make them feel *understood*.

#### The Reflection Formula:

> "So what I'm hearing is... [summarize emotional state]. 
> And what you really want is... [desired feeling]. 
> Does that sound right?"

#### Example:

> "So what I'm hearing is that your team has powerful AI tools, but there's this undercurrent of uncertainty — people aren't always sure 'the right way' to use them. And what you really want is that feeling of everyone being aligned and confident, without having to hold their hand through every decision. Does that capture it?"

**The feeling we create:** *Finally, someone gets it.*

---

### Phase 3: The Bridge (Painting the Transformation)

**Goal:** Help them *feel* the future state.

#### The Bridge Formula:

> "Imagine it's 3 months from now. 
> You're watching your team [specific scenario]. 
> What's different is [transformation]. 
> How does that feel?"

#### Example:

> "Imagine it's 3 months from now. It's Tuesday morning, and a junior engineer just joined yesterday. You see them in Slack: they've already shipped their first PR using one of your team's standard workflows. No one had to tell them how. It was just... there. 
> 
> What's different is that your team's wisdom isn't locked in docs or heads — it's encoded, automatic, and available. 
> 
> How does that feel?"

**Wait for them to describe the feeling.** That's the buying signal.

---

### Phase 4: The Demo (Feeling First, Features Second)

**Goal:** Create an emotional experience, not a product tour.

#### The Demo Script:

**Before showing anything:**
> "I'm going to show you something, but I want you to pay attention to how it *feels*, not what it *does*. Ready?"

**During the demo:**
- Narrate the *experience*, not the mechanics
- Use feeling words: "Notice how easy this feels..." "See how confident..."
- Pause after key moments: "What did that feel like?"

**After the demo:**
> "You just saw [feature], but what I really want to know: did you feel that sense of [target feeling]?"

#### Demo Flow Example (for `/record-demo`):

> "Picture this: Your PM asks for a product demo by EOD. It's 4 PM.
> 
> Old way: Panic. OBS. Export fails. 'I'll do it tomorrow.'
> 
> New way: [Type command]. 3 minutes. Done.
> 
> But here's what matters: How do you feel right now? Not 'that was fast' — but 'I actually know how to do this. I can handle these requests. I'm capable.'
> 
> That's what we're selling."

---

### Phase 5: The Close (Feelings-Based Commitment)

**Goal:** Get commitment based on emotional transformation, not logical features.

#### The Commitment Question:

> "If we could give your team that feeling of [desired state] — [confidence/flow/pride/etc.] — would it be worth exploring?"

#### Handling Objections (Feeling-First):

**"We don't have budget"**
> "I hear that. Let me ask: What's the emotional cost of *not* solving this? How does your team feel today, and how will they feel in 6 months if nothing changes?"

**"We need to evaluate other options"**
> "Absolutely. As you evaluate, I'd encourage you to pay attention to how each option makes you *feel*. Not just the features, but the feeling of using it. That's what your team will experience every day."

**"This seems complicated"**
> "I get that concern. Can I share what other teams tell us? On day 1, it feels new. By day 7, it feels obvious. By day 30, they can't imagine working without it. The complexity is upfront; the feeling is lasting."

**"We can build this ourselves"**
> "You absolutely can. Many teams do. The question is: how will your team feel while they're building it vs. using it? Building = more work, more decisions, more 'how should we do this?' Using = flow, confidence, shipping. What's the right feeling for where you are right now?"

## THE SALES TEAM MANIFESTO

### Our Non-Negotiables

1. **We leave people better than we found them**
   - Every call, every email, every interaction
   - Even if they don't buy, they feel understood

2. **We sell the feeling, not the feature**
   - Features are proof points
   - Feelings are the product

3. **We listen more than we talk**
   - 70% listening, 30% talking
   - The prospect's words are more powerful than ours

4. **We get specific about feelings**
   - Not "better" but "confident on Monday morning"
   - Not "easier" but "in flow state"

5. **We make it safe to say no**
   - Pressure kills feeling
   - Authenticity creates connection

## THE OBJECTION HANDLING PLAYBOOK

### Reframe Every Objection as a Feeling Question

| Objection | Reframe | Response |
|-----------|---------|----------|
| "Too expensive" | "What does 'expensive' feel like?" | "Help me understand — is this about the number, or the feeling of investing in something unproven?" |
| "Need to think about it" | "What are you feeling uncertain about?" | "Totally fair. What feeling is holding you back — is it risk, or timing, or something else?" |
| "Not a priority" | "What feels more urgent?" | "I hear you. What's the feeling that's making this wait — is it that things are okay enough, or that other fires are burning?" |
| "Need team buy-in" | "What will convince them?" | "What feeling do you think your team needs to experience to get excited about this?" |
| "Already have tools" | "How do those tools make you feel?" | "You do — and that's great. How do those tools make your team *feel* on a day-to-day basis?" |

## THE POST-SALE FEELING JOURNEY

### Onboarding: From Excitement to Confidence

**Week 1: The Honeymoon**
- Focus: Quick wins, immediate value
- Feeling: "This is cool!"

**Week 2-3: The Integration**
- Focus: Team adoption, workflow integration
- Feeling: "This is becoming natural"

**Week 4+: The Belonging**
- Focus: Custom skills, team patterns
- Feeling: "This is how we work"

### Check-ins: Measuring Feelings

Don't ask: "Are you using the skills?"
Ask: "How does your team feel about their workflow this week?"

Don't ask: "Any technical issues?"
Ask: "What's the best moment your team had with the skills this week?"
